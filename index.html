Ceramic Tiles

Ceramic tiles are made from natural clay and are hardened in a kiln at high temperatures. They are available in a variety of colors, shapes, sizes, and finishes, including matte and glossy.

Best Places to Use:
  • Kitchen Backsplash: Protects the walls from moisture, grease, and stains while offering a decorative element.
  • Bathroom Walls & Floors: Water-resistant properties make ceramic tiles perfect for areas prone to moisture.
  • Living Rooms & Hallways: While slightly less durable than porcelain, ceramic tiles can still be used in areas that don’t face heavy foot traffic.

Porcelain Tiles

Porcelain tiles are denser, harder, and less porous than ceramic tiles. These tiles are fired at higher temperatures, making them more durable and water-resistant.

Best Places to Use:
  • High-Traffic Areas: Such as hallways, foyers, and living rooms due to their durability.
  • Outdoor Patios: Porcelain tiles are often frost-proof and can withstand outdoor weather conditions.
  • Bathrooms & Wet Areas: Their water resistance makes them perfect for bathroom flooring and shower walls.

Mosaic Tiles

Mosaic tiles are small pieces of glass, stone, or ceramic that are usually assembled on a mesh backing. They are often used for decorative purposes to create intricate patterns.

Best Places to Use:
  • Bathroom Backsplashes: Adds a decorative, detailed touch behind sinks or around bathtubs.
  • Shower Floors & Walls: The small size and slip-resistant nature of mosaics make them ideal for wet, slippery areas.
  • Kitchen Backsplashes: Popular in kitchens for creating eye-catching, decorative backsplashes.

Vinyl Tiles

Vinyl tiles are synthetic and made from layers of PVC, making them flexible, affordable, and easy to install. They often mimic the appearance of more expensive materials, such as hardwood or stone.

Best Places to Use:
  • Bathrooms: Since they are 100% waterproof, they are perfect for bathrooms, laundry rooms, and basements.
  • Kitchens: Vinyl tiles provide a softer surface, reducing foot fatigue in areas where you stand for long periods.
  • Basements: Their waterproof nature and easy installation make them great for basements prone to moisture.
